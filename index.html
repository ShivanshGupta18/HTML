Coronavirus Help Page

What is Coronavirus?

Most people infected with the COVID-19 virus experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. Older people, and those with underlying medical problems like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and cancer are more likely to develop serious illness.The first case of COVID-19 was reported Dec. 1, 2019, and the cause was a then-new coronavirus later named SARS-CoV-2. SARS-CoV-2 may have originated in an animal and changed (mutated) so it could cause illness in humans. In the past, several infectious disease outbreaks have been traced to viruses originating in birds, pigs, bats and other animals that mutated to become dangerous to humans. Research continues, and more study may reveal how and why the coronavirus evolved to cause pandemic disease.

Coronavirus Symptoms

Fever, coughing, shortness of breath, trouble breathing, chills, body aches, headache, sore throat, congestion/runny nose, loss of smell or taste, nausea, diarrhea.The virus can lead to some fatal diseases like pneumonia, respiratory failure, heart problems, liver problems, septic shock, and even death.

Prevention Measures

If you don’t feel well, stay home. Even if you have mild symptoms like a headache and runny nose, stay in until you’re better.Cover your nose and mouth in public.Wash your hands often with soap and water or clean them with an alcohol-based sanitizer. Call the doctor if you have trouble breathing.

Emergerncy Situation

The number of people infected by COVID-19 changes every day.The transmission rate is relatively high. Early research has estimated that one person who has it can spread it to between 2 and 3.5 others.Till now 16.7 Crores cases have been reported worldwide out of which 34.6 Lakh people have died.Thererefore it is very necessary to stay at home.
